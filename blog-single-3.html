Should You Build a Website or an App First? A Guide for Indian SMEs

📅 Jan 2025 ⏱️ 6 min read ✍️ VPDroid Team

One of the most common questions we get from small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) in India is: "Should I build a website or a mobile app?" The answer isn't always straightforward. It depends on your business model, target audience, and budget. Here is a guide to help you make the right choice.

1. The Case for a Website

If your primary goal is to attract new customers, increase visibility, and establish credibility, a website should be your first priority. A responsive, SEO-optimized website acts as your digital storefront. It's universally accessible across all devices without requiring users to download anything. Whether customers are searching on Google or clicking a link on social media, a website is the easiest way for them to find and learn about your business.

2. When an App Makes Sense

Mobile apps are ideal for businesses that prioritize customer retention, frequent engagement, and personalized experiences. If your business relies on daily usage (like a habit-tracking app), e-commerce with loyalty programs, or requires access to native device features like the camera or GPS, an app is the way to go. Apps provide a dedicated space on a user's phone, sending push notifications that keep your brand top of mind.

3. Consider Your Budget and Resources

Developing a high-quality app generally requires a larger upfront investment and ongoing maintenance compared to a website. You have to consider development for multiple platforms (Android and iOS) and adhere to app store guidelines. If you have a limited budget, starting with a robust website is often the most cost-effective way to establish your digital presence.

4. The Progressive Web App (PWA) Alternative

Can't decide? A Progressive Web App might be the sweet spot. PWAs are web applications that look and feel like mobile apps. They can be installed on a user's home screen, work offline, and send push notifications, all while being accessed through a standard web browser. This approach offers the discoverability of a website with the engagement features of an app, making it a powerful solution for many SMEs.

5. The Verdict

For most Indian SMEs starting their digital journey, a website is the foundational step. It establishes trust and searchability. Once you have a steady stream of traffic and a loyal customer base, investing in a mobile app can take your user engagement and retention to the next level.
